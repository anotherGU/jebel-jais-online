Bear Grylls Explorers Camp – Full Guide, What to Expect & How to Prepare

Ready to push your limits the smart way? This guide walks you through time requirements, what’s included, gear to bring, safety rules, and a step-by-step flow so you know exactly what happens at Bear Grylls Explorers Camp before you arrive.

Short version: expect a skills-focused, instructor-led outdoor session that blends fun with real survival techniques — navigation, shelter building, knots, firecraft and teamwork.

Duration

~ 2.5–3 hours

Difficulty

Beginner → Moderate

Group Size

8–16 people

Minimum Age

8+ (with adult)
What’s Included

Exactly What You Get

Professional instructors certified to run outdoor skills sessions with clear safety briefings.

All required equipment for activities (knots, shelter, firecraft, basic navigation).

Brief photos allowed during non-safety-critical moments (ask your instructor first).

Completion highlight — you’ll leave with practical skills and great memories.

Checklist

What to Bring

Comfortable outdoor clothing you don’t mind getting dusty. No loose scarves or jewelry.

Closed-toe footwear with good grip (sneakers or light hiking shoes).

Water bottle (hydration is key). Sunscreen & sunglasses recommended.

Valid ID & booking confirmation (digital is fine).

Step-by-step

Flow of the Experience

01

Check-in & briefing (10–15 min): meet your instructor, sign a quick waiver, gear overview, safety rules.

02

Navigation & knots (25–35 min): compass basics, map orientation, key knots for shelter and hauling.

03

Shelter craft (25–35 min): building a simple, safe structure with limited resources and teamwork.

04

Firecraft (25–35 min): fire triangle, safe zones, supervised ignition and extinction protocols.

05

Final challenge (15–20 min): short group task combining skills you learned.

06

Wrap-up & tips (5–10 min): debrief, photos, hydration, next adventures to try.

Safety & Requirements

Good to Know Before You Book

  • Health

    Participants should be in general good health, able to walk on uneven ground, bend, kneel and lift light items.

  • Age

    Minimum 8+ with a supervising adult. Guardians sign for minors.

  • Clothing

    No heels, sandals, or loose accessories. Long hair tied back. Follow instructor directions at all times.

  • Weather

    Activities may adapt to weather/heat. Hydration breaks are planned; bring a personal bottle.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this suitable for complete beginners?

Yes. The camp is designed for first-timers. You’ll be guided step by step, with options to increase challenge if your group is experienced.

Do I need to bring any special gear?

No. All technical equipment is provided. Just arrive in comfortable clothing and closed-toe shoes, and bring water.

Can I take photos or videos?

Generally yes during non-critical moments. Please always ask your instructor — safety comes first.

How early should I arrive?

Plan to arrive 15–20 minutes before your slot to check in, use restrooms, and start on time.
